GENOME PROPERTIES

Proteomes for testing
Ten eukaryotes were chose, about a hundred bacteria and a dozen archaea.  For bacteria and archaea an example was chosen from each different order where a species had had its genome sequenced.  The species chosen, if not obvious, was the one with the lowest NCBI taxonomy ID on the assumption that the most well known organisms will have lower taxonomy IDs.  For each single-celled organism, a particular strain was chosen.  Usually, this was also the strain with the lowest NCBI taxonomy ID, but for some genome sequences, NCBI do not record the strain.  In these cases, the known strain with the lowest taxonomy ID was chosen.
